# 🧱 Unit 1: Algebra Foundations

Welcome to Unit 1! In this unit, we’ll build the mathematical foundation you'll need to succeed in Algebra and beyond. Think of this as sharpening your tools before you build something big.

Whether it’s simplifying fractions, working with percentages, or understanding how to factor numbers, these skills will help you see patterns, solve problems faster, and feel more confident in your math work.

---

## 🧮 What You’ll Learn

By the end of this unit, you’ll be able to:

- Add, subtract, multiply, and divide whole numbers, fractions, and decimals
- Use the correct **order of operations** (PEMDAS)
- Break numbers into **prime factors** using **factor trees**
- Find the **GCF (Greatest Common Factor)** and **LCM (Least Common Multiple)**
- Convert between **fractions**, **decimals**, and **percentages**
- Solve **proportions** and apply them to real-world problems
- Calculate **area and perimeter** for basic geometric shapes

---

## 📦 Topics in This Unit

### 1. Basic Computation
Review the four operations with whole numbers and how to apply them in word problems.

### 2. Prime Factorization & Factor Trees
Learn how to break down a number into its prime building blocks and draw **factor trees** to visualize it.

### 3. Order of Operations
Use **PEMDAS** to simplify expressions correctly:
> Parentheses → Exponents → Multiplication/Division → Addition/Subtraction

### 4. GCF & LCM
Discover how to:
- Find the **GCF** to simplify fractions and solve problems with shared factors
- Find the **LCM** to solve problems involving common multiples (like syncing schedules)

### 5. Fractions and Decimals
Understand how to:
- Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions and decimals
- Convert between the two forms easily

### 6. Proportions and Percentages
Solve real-life problems using:
- **Ratios and proportions** (e.g., recipes, maps)
- **Percentages** (e.g., tax, discounts, tips)

### 7. Area and Perimeter
Use formulas to find the **area** and **perimeter** of:
- Rectangles
- Triangles
- Parallelograms
- Trapezoids
